sazhinfed joins Doctors as a young prospect
📅 June 17, 2026

Doctors have signed 18-year-old rifler sazhinfed to their academy roster, the organization announced today. The emerging talent has been turning heads on the FACEIT platform with less than 200 hours in the game, already matching the team's all-time record rating.

The Russian organization has been actively scouting for fresh talent to reinforce their developmental pipeline, and sazhinfed quickly became the primary target after a string of standout performances in the FACEIT ladder. Despite his minimal playtime — just over 190 hours — the 18-year-old has demonstrated mechanical prowess that far exceeds his experience level.

According to sources close to the team, Doctors' coaching staff led by Mikhail 'adski_profi' Bortsov had been tracking sazhinfed's progress for several weeks before making their move. His ability to consistently deliver against seasoned opponents caught their attention, with the youngster showcasing exceptional crosshair placement and raw aim — qualities that many believe could translate seamlessly to the professional stage.

What truly sets sazhinfed apart, however, is his uncanny knack for clutch situations. Despite his limited understanding of the game's deeper tactical layers — a natural consequence of his short playtime — he repeatedly finds himself winning rounds that seem utterly unwinnable. Whether it's a 1v3 or a 1v4, the young rifler has a habit of pulling off the improbable, often leaving opponents and spectators alike in disbelief. It's this blend of raw firepower and fearless decision-making in high-pressure moments that has drawn comparisons to some of the region's most exciting prospects.

The crown jewel of sazhinfed's FACEIT career so far came when he posted a staggering 2.49 rating on a single map, tying the record previously set by Doctors' main roster star Dew1erMode — an achievement that immediately put him on the radar of multiple CIS organizations. This performance, combined with his rapid improvement trajectory, convinced Doctors to act decisively to secure his signature.

While sazhinfed is expected to begin his journey in the academy lineup, team officials have hinted at the possibility of integration into the main roster should his development maintain its current pace. The organization views him as a "future superstar" and is committed to investing the necessary time and resources to cultivate his raw talent.

Doctors' captain, Vadim 'Dew1erMode' Chernov, shared his thoughts on the signing:

"sazhinfed is exactly the kind of player we want to bring into our system — hungry, naturally gifted, and insanely dedicated. He has under 200 hours in the game, but he's already competing at a level that most players only reach after thousands of hours. His crosshair placement and raw aim are elite — it's honestly scary to think where he'll be in a year. He has absolutely monstrous potential.

That said, he's still raw. His game sense isn't there yet — he doesn't have the hours to read the game properly — but his aim just bails him out of everything. He's mechanically gifted in ways you can't teach. He also needs to work on his mentality; he can get heated and tilted, and sometimes it affects his decision-making. But that's exactly what our system is for — we'll help him channel that fire into focus. Once we smooth out those edges, I truly believe he can become one of the best." — Vadim 'Dew1erMode' Chernov

Before joining Doctors, sazhinfed spent the bulk of his short CS2 journey in the FACEIT hub, where he accumulated over 150 matches with a win rate far exceeding 50% and a peak Elo of 1,219. His ability to consistently out-aim opponents and his fearless approach to engagements made him a sought-after prospect, but Doctors moved swiftly to secure his signature before any other organization could formalize an offer.

The young rifler will now work closely with the coaching staff to transition from the FACEIT environment to structured team play, with the organization's academy program designed to accelerate his growth. Team insiders suggest that sazhinfed will be given opportunities to showcase his abilities in upcoming tournaments, potentially as early as next month.
